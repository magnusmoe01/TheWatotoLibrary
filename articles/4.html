Our Vision

Partnering with WWQA

Posted on November 5, 2022

The Watoto Library has started a partnership with the World Water Quality Alliance, a sub-division of the United Nations Environment Programme. The goal of the partnership is to do cleanups around Kibera with the aim of improving the water quality of the community.


Kibera River

Kibera suffers from a lack of access to basic needs, such as clean water and a sewage system. The Kibera river is a source of water for the community living near it, but unfortunately, it has faced numerous challenges that have affected its quality and availability. Over time, the river has become polluted due to human waste being dumped into it, which has led to cholera development and health problems for those who rely on it.


WWQA Conference

Brandon was invited to attend the annual WWQA conference in Konstanz, Germany as a sponsored participant, where he shared his expertise on the water and river challenges facing the Kibera community. Here, he outlined the issues and proposed potential solutions. The conference attendees, which included industry leaders and experts in the field, were deeply saddened by the current state of the Kibera river and began discussing ways to take action and improve the water situation. Brandon appreciated the opportunity to raise awareness about the challenges facing his community and we look forward to changing it.
